About Us

Learn More About Us

STEM High School for Boys - STEM KFS, ranked as Egypt's best high school, was established through a partnership between the U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID), World Learning, and the ECASE project. The school aims to provide technical assistance to the Ministry of Education in Egypt and focuses on scientific, mathematical, and technological fields. It caters to high-potential students who exhibit strong academic skills. At STEM High School, students benefit from a unique educational system led by professors engaged in post-teacher studies. The curriculum emphasizes critical inquiry, research, problem-solving skills, intellectual curiosity, and social responsibility. As the first school of its kind in Egypt, STEM High School welcomes applicants from various governorates, including Cairo, Giza, Qalubia, Fayoum, and Bani Souwaif, as well as neighboring cities.

Our Targeted Students

With a highly competitive admission process, STEM KFS accepts approximately 150 students each year out of a pool of 5000 applicants. The school specifically seeks out aspiring students who possess a strong passion and talent in the fields of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM). Prospective students who wish to join STEM October are expected to demonstrate a commitment to ongoing learning, self-improvement, and a willingness to contribute their knowledge to the school community.
ELIGIBILTY CRITERIA
To gain admission to STEM KFS, applicants must fulfill one of the following requirements:

  • To have a total grade of more than 98% in the 9th Grade, and to have a grade of 100% in one of three Subjects: Maths, Science, and English.
  • To have a total grade of more than 95% in the 9th Grade, and to have a grade of 100% in two of three Subjects: Maths, Science, and English.

The eligibility criteria for admission into STEM KFS are subject to annual variation; however, they have remained consistent over the past two years and are expected to continue for the foreseeable future.

Upon meeting one of the aforementioned conditions, applicants proceed to undergo four additional acceptance tests. These tests aim to assess the applicants' proficiency in the respective subject areas: English, Mathematics, Science, and IQ. The tests are administered nationwide, ensuring a standardized evaluation process. Subsequently, students are ranked based on their performance in these four tests, with the top 150 candidates being accepted into STEM KFS.
